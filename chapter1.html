Introduction to Microorganisms

Microorganisms are very small living organisms that cannot be seen with the naked eye. A microscope is required to observe them. Microorganisms are found in air, water, soil, and inside living organisms.

These are some of the applications of beneficial microorganisms in our daily lives.

Agriculture

Disease Prevention and Treatment Caused by Microorganisms

Aseptic Technique

Aseptic techniques play an important role in the prevention and treatment of diseases caused by microorganisms. These techniques help prevent the spread of pathogens and reduce the risk of infection.

1. Sterilisation

The process of destroying all forms of microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, and spores.

2. Boiling

Using high temperature to kill or reduce the number of microorganisms in water or objects.

3. Use of Antiseptics

Applying chemicals on living tissues such as skin or wounds to inhibit or kill pathogens.

4. Use of Disinfectants

Using strong chemical agents on non-living surfaces to destroy microorganisms.

5. Use of Radiation

Exposing objects to radiation such as ultraviolet (UV) light to kill microorganisms and maintain sterility.
