Introduction to Space Technology

Space technology refers to the application of science and technology to explore and utilize outer space. It involves the use of rockets, spacecraft, satellites, and space stations. It plays an important role in improving human life on Earth. It helps in communication, weather forecasting, navigation, scientific research, and understanding the universe.

Satellites

A satellite is an object that orbits around a planet such as the Earth. Satellites can be divided into natural satellites and artificial satellites. Artificial satellites are launched into space using rockets and are designed for specific purposes.

Global Positioning System (GPS)

The Global Positioning System (GPS) is a satellite-based navigation system that allows users to determine their exact location, speed, and time anywhere on Earth. It is widely used in vehicles, smartphones, shipping, aviation, and even scientific research. GPS functions by receiving signals from multiple satellites in space to calculate accurate positioning.

The GPS system is divided into three main segments, each playing a critical role in providing accurate navigation and timing information. These are the three main segments:
