CHAPTER 7:
LIGHT & OPTICS

Introduction to Light

Light is a form of energy that enables us to see objects. It travels in straight lines and can undergo reflection, refraction, and dispersion. The study of light is known as optics.

Characteristics of Light
  • Light travels in straight lines.
  • Light can be reflected by surfaces.
  • Light can be refracted when passing through different media.
  • Light travels at a very high speed.

Reflection of Light

Reflection occurs when light strikes a surface and bounces back. A plane mirror produces a clear image due to regular reflection.

Laws of Reflection
  • The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
  • The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane.

Refraction of Light

Refraction occurs when light travels from one medium to another of different optical density, causing a change in speed and direction.
