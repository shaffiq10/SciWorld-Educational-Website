CHAPTER 6: ELECTROCHEMICAL

6.1 Electrochemical Cells

Electrochemistry studies the relationship between electrical energy and chemical reactions. It explains how chemical energy can be converted into electrical energy and vice versa.

Electrolytic Cell

Uses electrical energy to drive non-spontaneous chemical reactions. Commonly used in electroplating and metal extraction.

Chemical Cell

Converts chemical energy into electrical energy via spontaneous reactions. Examples include dry cells and batteries.

Electrolysis

Decomposition of electrolytes using electrical energy. Ions move to electrodes and react chemically.

Factors Affecting Electrolysis

  1. Position of Ions: Ions lower in the electrochemical series are more easily discharged.
  1. Concentration of Electrolyte: Higher concentration ions are more likely to be discharged.
  1. Type of Electrode: Inert electrodes do not react, active electrodes may participate in reaction.

6.2 Chemical Cells

Simple Chemical Cell

Two metals like zinc and copper in an electrolyte. The more reactive metal is the anode (oxidation), the less reactive is the cathode. Electrons flow through an external circuit to produce electrical energy.
